Scientific Reports · Dec 17, 2021
Functional Random Forests for Curve Response
Introduces a novel functional random forests (FunFor) approach to model functional data response that is densely and regularly measured. FunFor predicts curve responses for new observations and selects important variables from large sets of scalar predictors. It detects complex nonlinear and high-order interactions without parametric or distributional assumptions, and consistently outperformed existing methods across eight simulation settings and one real-data analysis. An R package is available on GitHub.

Book Recommendation

DATA FEMINISM
Recommended Read Open Access
Data Feminism
Catherine D'Ignazio & Lauren F. Klein · MIT Press, 2020
A landmark work examining how power and bias shape data collection, analysis, and modeling. For statisticians and actuaries, this book reframes model fairness not as a PR concern but as a model validity problem — our GLMs aren't neutral, and variable selection embeds assumptions about who matters.
Why it matters for quant work
Historical claims data reflects who was insured, on what terms — your model inherits that history. Removing a variable doesn't remove its influence; correlated proxies absorb the signal.
Key application
Disparate impact testing, causal DAG analysis, and orthogonalisation — practical tools to audit rating variables for proxy discrimination in pricing models.
